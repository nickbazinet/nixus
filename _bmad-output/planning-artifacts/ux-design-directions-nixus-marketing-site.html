Nixus Marketing Site — Hero Direction Mockups

Three hero treatments using the locked design tokens. Same content, same components — only the hero composition varies. Below each hero, a simplified shared canvas (features → footer) shows context.

A — Linear-style split B — Raycast-style centered C — Demo-as-hero (dark)
Variant A

Linear-style split hero

Headline + subhead + dual CTA on the left, idle preview of the AI demo on the right. Tight, text-forward, animation as supporting element. Closest to Linear's marketing pattern.

Strengths: Headline does the heavy lifting; demo is visible above the fold but doesn't dominate. Great for the 5-second scanner. Clear hierarchy.

Trade-offs: Demo is small at this size — the magic moment is somewhat muted until the visitor scrolls into the full demo section below. Bets on copy carrying the pitch.

Variant B

Raycast-style centered, full demo below

Centered hero with a punchy headline, single primary CTA, and a small alt-OS link. The full AI demo gets its own section directly below the hero — bigger, full-bleed, the moment it deserves.

Strengths: Hero feels confident and composed. Demo gets full real estate when it appears, hitting harder. Single CTA reduces decision friction. Best emotional arc.

Trade-offs: Demo is below the fold — visitors who don't scroll past the hero never see the magic. Relies more on the headline to earn the scroll.

Variant C

Demo-as-hero (dark backdrop)

The demo IS the hero, on a dark slate backdrop that puts maximum focus on the categorization moment. Headline and CTA live above the demo. Most aggressive bet on the demo as the conversion lever.

Strengths: Maximum craft signal — the demo gets premiere placement. Dark backdrop makes the screenshot pop and feels editorial. Strongest first impression.

Trade-offs: Heaviest first paint (large demo above the fold). Dark hero diverges most from the desktop app's calm-white aesthetic — weakens visual continuity. Risk of over-promising if the actual app feels less polished.
